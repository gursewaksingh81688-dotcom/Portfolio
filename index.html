About Me

I am an M.Sc. Physics graduate from SVKM's Mithibai College, Mumbai University, passionate about understanding the fundamental laws of nature through theoretical physics and scientific computing. My interests span particle physics, computational astrophysics, cosmology, gravitation, and quantum field theory. I enjoy combining analytical methods with numerical simulations to investigate challenging problems in modern physics, and I am currently seeking a PhD position where I can contribute to research in theoretical and computational physics.

For my Master's thesis at IIT Hyderabad, under Dr. Divya Sachdeva, I built Python-based Monte Carlo simulations to model how Weakly Interacting Massive Particles (WIMPs) are captured and thermalised inside the Sun. The work used spin-independent (Operator 1) WIMP–nucleon interactions within the non-relativistic effective field theory (NREFT) framework, tracking particle trajectories, scattering events, and energy loss through realistic solar models.

Before that, I spent the summer of 2025 at the Physical Research Laboratory (PRL), Ahmedabad, writing simulations to understand when and why presolar silicate grains survive — or don't — the extreme conditions inside supernova remnants.

I am now actively applying for PhD positions in particle physics, dark matter, and computational astrophysics. If your group works on direct detection phenomenology, dark matter theory, or related computational problems, I would be very glad to hear from you.

Currently Extending my Monte Carlo simulations of dark matter capture and thermalization as independent research, while applying for PhD programmes.

May – Jun 2025 Research Internship

Destruction Mechanism of Presolar Grains

Physical Research Laboratory (PRL), Ahmedabad

Wrote Python simulations to investigate how presolar silicate grains are destroyed — and how some survive — in the extreme environments produced by supernova explosions. Studied the effects of grain size, gas velocity, and post-shock temperature on survival fractions using published astrophysical models. Results were presented as a poster at the 5th METMESS Conference, IIST, 2025.
